Renewable Energy : A quick overview

Created by Nikolai Nekrutenko

Introduction

Renewable energy is a very good, reliable way of generating electricity without creating almost any pollution.

There are several major types of renewable energy sources:

  • Hydro
  • Wind
  • Solar
  • Geothermal

The relative use of each type looks like this:

Hydro

Hydroelectric energy is a type of energy derived from the kinetic motion of water because of gravity. It accounts for around 81% of power generation of renewable energy, and it accounts for 16.3% of all electricity globally. Hydro electric power produced about 3503 Twh of electricity in 2014. Which provides power for about 380,000,000 homes.

A hydroelectric power plant

Wind

Wind power is a type of energy that works by generating energy from the wind, the wind spins an electric generator. It accounts for about 13% of all electricity generated in renewable energy, and for about 2.82% of all energy generated globally. It generated about 550 TWh in 2014. It provides power for about 60,000,000 homes.

A wind farm

Solar

Solar energy works by generating energy from the sun, which splits silicone atoms, that generates electricity. It accounts for about 4% of energy produced of renewable energy, and for about 1.08% of all electricity generated globally. It generated about 189.5 TWh in 2014. It provides power for about 20,500,000 homes.

A solar farm

Geothermal

Geothermal energy is a type of energy that is derived from heat inside the earth. The heat spins a turbine that is linked to a generator that generates power. It accounts for 2% of power generation of renewable energy, and it accounts for 0.54% of all the global energy generated. It generated about 79.7 TWh of electricity in 2014. It provides power for about 8,500,000 homes.

A geothermal power plant
